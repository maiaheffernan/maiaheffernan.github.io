3. **Alex**, last week you mentioned I should start coming up with some hypotheses, so here is what I have so far (this is nothing new, really, just me thinking through what we have all been getting at for a while)
- I think hypoxia in Penn Cove is fundamentally a residence time story. Aurora has shown that residence time is a primary driver of hypoxia, and Dakota’s plots revealing a two-cell circulation pattern, which I'm also beginning to tease apart from the December data, suggest that the back bay and south bay become hypoxic simply because water stays there longer. Additionally, if a counterclockwise circulation exists in the cove, it would explain why hypoxic water accumulates at the tail end of that circulation cell along the south length before eventually being advected out.
    - It would be really cool to be able to characterize these circulation cells in the cove!
- Aurora has also shown that stratification is important for hypoxia dynamics. I think this will be relevant in Penn Cove given the direct influence of the Skagit River, and I think it will be the second-order driver of hypoxia after the residence time.
    - I think the mooring design is well set-up to measure these things. 
- I would also like to at some point make an oxygen budget for the cove. I am not quite sure what this will look like given that there will be a master’s student in David’s lab that will be working on this, but it is on my mind as a potential collaboration moving forward.
